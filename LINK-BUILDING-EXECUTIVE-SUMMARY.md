## ‚ö†Ô∏è Key Risks & Mitigation

### Risk 1: Low Outreach Response Rate (<20%)
**Impact**: Fewer backlinks than targeted
**Mitigation**:
- Personalize every email (no copy-paste)
- Follow up 2-3 times per target
- Offer value (free photos, exclusive info, free stay)
- A/B test subject lines
**Contingency**: Focus on directories (80% success rate) if outreach stalls

---

### Risk 2: Time/Resource Constraints
**Impact**: Campaign execution slower than planned
**Mitigation**:
- Prioritize Tier 1 targets only (20% effort, 80% results)
- Batch similar tasks (all directory submissions in one session)
- Use provided templates (minimize writing time)
- Delegate to volunteers (task checklist provided)
**Contingency**: Extend timeline from 90 days to 6 months

---

### Risk 3: Budget Limitations
**Impact**: Cannot use paid tactics (influencers, PR wire)
**Mitigation**:
- 80% of strategy is zero-cost
- Free alternatives exist for all paid tools
- Barter partnerships (free stay instead of money)
**Contingency**: Execute Tier 1 (zero budget) plan only‚Äîstill effective

---

### Risk 4: Wikipedia Edits Reverted
**Impact**: Cannot update Wikipedia page directly
**Mitigation**:
- Declare conflict of interest on Talk page
- Provide high-quality third-party sources
- Make incremental improvements (not major rewrites)
- Let neutral editors implement suggestions
**Contingency**: Suggest improvements on Talk page, don't edit directly

---

### Risk 5: Negative Reviews / Reputation Issues
**Impact**: Bad reviews damage credibility
**Mitigation**:
- Monitor Google/TripAdvisor daily
- Respond professionally within 24 hours
- Flag fake reviews to platforms
- Proactively collect 100+ positive reviews (dilute negatives)
**Contingency**: Crisis communication plan (apologize, fix issue, offer resolution)
